National Organizations

These organizations work specifically with Trisomy 13 and related conditions at the national level. They offer family support, research funding, educational materials, and connections to specialists.

SOFT — Support Organization for Trisomy 18, 13 and Related Disorders

The primary national nonprofit for families of children with Trisomy 13 and 18. Runs an annual conference, regional chapter network, and provides care guidelines for medical teams.

trisomy.org
1-800-716-SOFT (7638)
barbsoft@aol.com

Trisomy 13 Support Foundation

Dedicated specifically to Trisomy 13 families. Offers family support networks, medical referrals, and educational resources for parents navigating a new diagnosis.

trisomy13support.org

National Organization for Rare Disorders (NORD)

Provides patient assistance grants, a free rare disease registry, and advocacy support for individuals with conditions including Patau Syndrome.

rarediseases.org

March of Dimes

Funds research into birth defects and chromosomal conditions. Offers NICU family support programs and grief/loss resources at many participating hospitals.

Special Schools & Early Intervention Programs

Under federal law, children with disabilities qualify for free services from birth. Here's what's available for our child.

Early Intervention — Birth to Age 3 (Federal, all states)

Under IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act), every child with a diagnosed disability from birth to age 3 qualifies for free early intervention services: physical therapy, occupational therapy, speech-language therapy, and developmental support.

Financial Assistance — Local & National

Medical care for a child with Patau Syndrome is expensive. These programs can help offset costs.

Medicaid / [Your State] CHIP

Children with serious medical conditions typically qualify for Medicaid or the Children's Health Insurance Program based on disability status, regardless of household income. This can dramatically reduce out-of-pocket medical costs.

medicaid.gov
1-800-318-2596

SSI — Supplemental Security Income

Children with significant disabilities may qualify for monthly SSI payments through Social Security to help cover living and medical expenses.

ssa.gov
1-800-772-1213

Ronald McDonald House Charities

Provides free or reduced-cost housing near children's hospitals for families who must travel for treatment. Has locations in many cities nationwide.
